Understanding Changes in Crime Patterns During COVID-19

This data story explores the changes in crime patterns in San Francisco during the COVID-19 pandemic. The dataset used for this analysis includes records of "Personal Focus Crimes," which are specific categories of offenses that law enforcement agencies prioritize for enforcement and prevention efforts.

The data spans from 2010 to 2021, capturing various crime incidents reported in the city. By analyzing this dataset, we aim to uncover insights into how crime rates and types have shifted during the pandemic, highlighting any significant trends or anomalies that emerged during this unprecedented time.

Through a series of visualizations, we will illustrate the impact of COVID-19 on crime in San Francisco, providing a clearer understanding of the evolving landscape of public safety in the city.
